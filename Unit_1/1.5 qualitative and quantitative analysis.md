## **Qualitative and Quantitative Risk Analysis**

### **Introduction**

Risk analysis is a core part of risk management used to identify, evaluate, and prioritize risks. It helps organizations understand potential threats and decide suitable controls. Risk analysis is mainly of two types: **Qualitative** and **Quantitative**.

---

## **1. Qualitative Risk Analysis**

**Qualitative risk analysis** evaluates risk based on **descriptive scales** such as _High, Medium, or Low_. It relies on **expert judgment, experience, and observation** rather than numerical data.

### **Key Characteristics**

- Uses **non-numeric values**
    
- Easy and quick to perform
    
- Based on interviews, questionnaires, and risk matrices
    
- Useful when exact data is not available
    

### **Advantages**

- Simple and easy to understand
    
- Requires less time and cost
    
- Suitable for initial risk assessment
    

### **Limitations**

- Subjective in nature
    
- Less accurate than quantitative analysis
    

---

## **2. Quantitative Risk Analysis**

**Quantitative risk analysis** measures risk using **numerical values** such as monetary loss, probability, and frequency.

### **Key Characteristics**

- Uses **statistical and mathematical models**
    
- Provides measurable results in financial terms
    
- Common terms: **SLE, ARO, ALE**
    

### **Important Formulas**

- **SLE (Single Loss Expectancy)** = Asset Value Ã— Exposure Factor
    
- **ARO (Annual Rate of Occurrence)** = Frequency of incident per year
    
- **ALE (Annual Loss Expectancy)** = SLE Ã— ARO

- More accurate and objective

- Requires reliable data
