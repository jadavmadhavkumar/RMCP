## **Goals of Risk Management**

**Risk Management** is the systematic process of identifying, analyzing, and controlling risks to protect an organization‚Äôs assets, information, and operations.

### **Main Goals of Risk Management**

1. **Identify Potential Risks**  
    To recognize threats and vulnerabilities that may affect information systems, people, processes, or assets.
    
2. **Reduce Loss and Damage**  
    To minimize financial loss, data breaches, system failures, and operational disruptions.
    
3. **Ensure Business Continuity**  
    To keep critical business operations running even during incidents such as cyber-attacks, system failures, or disasters.
    
4. **Protect Information Assets**  
    To safeguard confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) of data.
    
5. **Support Decision Making**  
    To help management make informed decisions by understanding risk levels and acceptable risks.
    
6. **Optimize Resource Utilization**  
    To apply security controls where they are most needed instead of wasting resources.
    
7. **Ensure Legal and Regulatory Compliance**  
    To meet laws, standards, and regulations and avoid penalties.
    
8. **Improve Organizational Resilience**  
    To prepare the organization to respond, recover, and adapt to risks effectively.

## **Scenario (Exam-Friendly Example)**

**Scenario: Online Banking System**

A bank identifies the risk of **phishing attacks** that may steal customer login credentials.

- **Risk Identification:** Phishing emails targeting customers
    
- **Risk Analysis:** High impact (financial loss, loss of trust)
    
- **Risk Treatment:** Two-factor authentication, user awareness training, email filtering
    
- **Result:** Reduced fraud, protected customer data, uninterrupted banking services

## **Conclusion**

The primary goal of risk management is **not to eliminate all risks**, but to **identify, analyze, and control risks to an acceptable level** so that organizations can operate securely, efficiently, and confidently.
