## **Writing Audit Response**

### **Introduction**

An **audit response** is a **formal written reply** prepared by management after an audit.  
It addresses the **audit findings, observations, and recommendations** and explains **corrective actions**, timelines, and responsible persons.

Audit responses are an important part of **risk management, compliance, and governance**.

---

### **Objectives of Writing an Audit Response**

- Acknowledge audit findings
    
- Demonstrate accountability and transparency
    
- Describe corrective and preventive actions
    
- Reduce identified risks
    
- Ensure regulatory and policy compliance
    

---

### **Key Components of an Effective Audit Response**

1. **Acknowledgement of Finding**
    
    - Accept or clarify the audit observation
        
    - Show understanding of the issue
        
2. **Root Cause Analysis**
    
    - Explain why the issue occurred
        
    - Identify process, technical, or human causes
        
3. **Risk Impact Statement**
    
    - Explain potential impact if not fixed
        
    - Security, financial, operational, or compliance risk
        
4. **Corrective Action Plan**
    
    - Steps taken or planned to fix the issue
        
    - Include technical and administrative controls
        
5. **Preventive Measures**
    
    - Actions to avoid recurrence
        
    - Policy updates, training, monitoring
        
6. **Ownership and Responsibility**
    
    - Name department or role responsible
        
7. **Timeline**
    
    - Expected completion date
        
8. **Evidence and Documentation**
    
    - Proof such as logs, screenshots, policies
        

---

### **Characteristics of a Good Audit Response**

- Clear and professional
    
- Honest and factual
    
- Action-oriented
    
- Time-bound
    
- Aligned with organizational policies

## **Scenario Example (Very Exam-Friendly)**

### **Scenario: Audit of an Organization’s IT System**

**Audit Finding:**  
User accounts of resigned employees were not disabled on time.

---

### **Sample Audit Response**

**Acknowledgement:**  
We acknowledge the audit observation regarding delayed deactivation of user accounts.

**Root Cause:**  
The issue occurred due to lack of coordination between the HR and IT departments and absence of a formal off-boarding checklist.

**Risk Impact:**  
Unauthorized access by former employees could lead to data leakage, security breaches, and compliance violations.

**Corrective Action:**  
All inactive user accounts have been reviewed and immediately disabled.  
A centralized user access review has been conducted.

**Preventive Measures:**

- A formal employee exit process has been implemented
    
- HR-IT notification workflow automated
    
- Quarterly access reviews scheduled
    

**Responsibility:**  
IT Security Manager and HR Department

**Timeline:**  
Process implemented within 30 days

**Evidence:**  
Updated access control policy and user access logs attached.

---

### **Importance of Writing Audit Response**

- Improves security posture
    
- Strengthens internal controls
    
- Builds auditor confidence
    
- Supports continuous improvement
    
- Reduces future audit findings

Writing an audit response is a **critical activity in risk management and compliance**.  
A well-written response not only addresses audit findings but also ensures **long-term risk mitigation and process improvement**.
