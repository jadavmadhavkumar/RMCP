# **Risk Management Artifacts**

## **Definition**

**Risk management artifacts** are the **documents, records, and outputs** created during the risk management process.  
They provide **evidence, structure, traceability, and accountability** for identifying, analyzing, mitigating, and monitoring risks in an organization.

---

## **Purpose of Risk Management Artifacts**

- Document identified risks and decisions
    
- Support **audit, compliance, and governance**
    
- Enable **consistent risk handling**
    
- Communicate risk status to stakeholders
    
- Track risk treatment and progress over time
    

---

## **Key Risk Management Artifacts**

### **1. Risk Register**

- Central document listing all identified risks
    
- Contains:
    
    - Risk description
        
    - Likelihood and impact
        
    - Risk owner
        
    - Risk rating
        
    - Mitigation status
        

üìå _Most important artifact_

---

### **2. Risk Assessment Report**

- Documents the results of risk analysis
    
- Includes:
    
    - Scope and methodology
        
    - Identified threats and vulnerabilities
        
    - Impact analysis
        
    - Risk prioritization
        

Used by management for decision-making.

---

### **3. Risk Treatment / Mitigation Plan**

- Describes how risks will be handled
    
- Includes:
    
    - Mitigation actions
        
    - Responsible person
        
    - Timeline
        
    - Required resources
        

Risk treatment options:

- Avoid
    
- Mitigate
    
- Transfer
    
- Accept
    

---

### **4. Risk Acceptance Document**

- Formal approval to **accept a risk** without further treatment
    
- Signed by **risk owner or senior management**
    
- Includes justification and acceptance period
    

---

### **5. Policy Exception Document**

- Records exceptions where security policies are not followed
    
- Includes:
    
    - Reason for exception
        
    - Compensating controls
        
    - Expiry date
        

---

### **6. Audit Findings and Response Report**

- Documents audit results
    
- Includes:
    
    - Identified gaps
        
    - Management response
        
    - Corrective actions
        

---

### **7. Residual Risk Report**

- Shows risk remaining after controls are applied
    
- Helps decide whether additional controls are required
    

---

### **8. Risk Metrics and Dashboards**

- Visual representation of risk status
    
- Examples:
    
    - High-risk count
        
    - Open vs closed risks
        
    - Risk trends
        

Used by senior leadership.

## **Nice & Easy Scenario (Exam-Friendly)**

### **Scenario: E-Commerce Company**

An **e-commerce company** performs a risk assessment for its **online payment system**.

### **Step 1: Risk Register**

- Risk: Payment data breach
    
- Likelihood: Medium
    
- Impact: High
    
- Risk Owner: IT Security Manager
    

---

### **Step 2: Risk Assessment Report**

- Identifies weak encryption and outdated software
    
- Concludes risk level is **High**
    

---

### **Step 3: Risk Mitigation Plan**

- Upgrade encryption standards
    
- Apply security patches
    
- Implement multi-factor authentication
    

---

### **Step 4: Risk Acceptance**

- Minor risk of short downtime accepted by management during upgrades
    

---

### **Step 5: Audit Report**

- External audit confirms compliance after mitigation
    

---

### **Step 6: Residual Risk Report**

- Residual risk categorized as **Low**
    

This shows **how multiple risk management artifacts work together**.

## **Importance of Risk Management Artifacts**

- Provide **traceability and accountability**
    
- Support **regulatory compliance** (ISO 27001, NIST)
    
- Improve organizational risk maturity
    
- Help in audits and reviews

## **Conclusion**

Risk management artifacts are essential for a **systematic, transparent, and auditable risk management process**.  
They transform risk management from an **activity** into a **well-governed program**.
