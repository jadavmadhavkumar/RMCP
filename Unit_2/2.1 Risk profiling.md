## **Risk Profiling**

### **Definition**

Risk profiling is the process of **identifying, analyzing, and prioritizing risks** based on their **likelihood** and **impact** on an organization.  
It helps management understand **which risks are most critical** and need immediate attention.

---

### **Purpose of Risk Profiling**

- To identify **key risks** affecting assets, systems, and processes
    
- To **prioritize risks** for treatment
    
- To support **decision-making** in risk mitigation
    
- To align security efforts with **business objectives**
    

---

### **Key Components of Risk Profiling**

1. **Asset Identification** – Identify valuable assets (data, systems, people)
    
2. **Threat Identification** – Identify possible threats (hackers, malware, disasters)
    
3. **Vulnerability Identification** – Identify weaknesses in systems or processes
    
4. **Likelihood Assessment** – How often the risk may occur
    
5. **Impact Assessment** – Damage caused if the risk occurs
    
6. **Risk Rating** – Assigning risk level (High / Medium / Low)
    

---

### **Risk Profiling Process (Easy Steps)**

1. Identify assets
    
2. Identify threats and vulnerabilities
    
3. Assess likelihood and impact
    
4. Calculate risk level
    
5. Rank and document risks in a **risk profile**
    

---

### **Scenario (Exam-Friendly Example)**

**Scenario: Online Banking System**

An organization runs an **online banking application**.

- **Asset:** Customer financial data
    
- **Threat:** Cyber attacker
    
- **Vulnerability:** Weak password policy
    
- **Likelihood:** High (frequent login attempts)
    
- **Impact:** High (financial loss and reputation damage)
    

**Risk Profile Result:**  
This risk is categorized as **High Risk** and must be addressed immediately by enforcing **strong authentication and security controls**.

---

### **Advantages of Risk Profiling**

- Helps focus on **high-priority risks**
    
- Improves **security planning and control selection**
    
- Reduces financial and operational losses
    
- Supports compliance and governance
    

---

### **Conclusion**

Risk profiling provides a **structured view of organizational risks**, enabling effective risk management by prioritizing risks based on their **business impact** and **likelihood**.

---

### **Memory Tip (One-Line to Remember)**

**“Risk profiling = Identify → Analyze → Prioritize risks.”**
